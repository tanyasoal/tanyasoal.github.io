Answer: 

In reported speech, you have to change the order of part of speech in a sentence. This rules applies in both sentence and question. Here's the example: 

sentence: He said 'I have no time for this trivial things.'
reported: He said that he had no time for that trivial things. 

normal question: She asked 'where is my car?'
reported: She asked where her car was. 

yes/no question: She asked 'is he that dumb?'
reported: She asked if / whether he was that dumb. 

Here's the answer for the exercise above: 
1. b. where his wallet was
2. b. Where I was
3. a. how many children he has.
4. a. How close we are
5. b. what she said
6. a. What our children say
7. b. what he said.
8. b. what you should do
9. b. he is
10. a. where we should leave.
